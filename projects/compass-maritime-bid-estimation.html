Compass Maritime Service - Bid Price Estimation

Sep 2023 - Nov 2023 | Case Study Analysis

Challenge & Objective

Based on a Harvard Business Review case study, this project tackled the challenge of estimating optimal bid prices for maritime services using historical pricing data. The objective was to develop multiple regression models across different platforms (R, Python, Excel) and validate their accuracy through statistical testing.

Approach & Methodology

1. Multi-Platform Modeling

Estimated ship bid prices using multiple linear regression models implemented in three platforms: R, Python, and Excel. This cross-platform approach enabled validation of statistical assumptions and ensured robust, reproducible results.

2. Statistical Validation

Applied rigorous statistical testing including R-squared analysis, ANOVA (Analysis of Variance), and factor analysis to test model efficiency. Validated that all models achieved statistical significance and accurately captured pricing patterns.

3. Case Study Implementation

Based on Harvard Business Review case study methodology, developed pricing models accounting for historical data and multiple influencing factors. Compared results across R, Python, and Excel to ensure consistency and reliability.

Results & Impact

  • Multi-Model Validation: Successfully developed and validated consistent results across R, Python, and Excel platforms
  • Statistical Testing: Used R-squared value, ANOVA, and factor analysis to comprehensively test model efficiency
  • Cross-Platform Consistency: Confirmed statistical assumptions and model robustness across different implementations
  • Pricing Insights: Identified key factors driving maritime service bid prices for strategic decision-making

Tech Stack

R Python Excel Linear Regression ANOVA Statistical Analysis RStudio Scikit-learn

Key Learnings

  • Multiple linear regression remains powerful for interpretable pricing models
  • Cross-platform implementation helps validate statistical assumptions and results
  • ANOVA and F-tests are essential for statistical validation of regression models
  • R-squared and adjusted R-squared provide complementary model evaluation metrics
  • Excel, despite limitations, is valuable for transparent, stakeholder-friendly modeling
  • Real-world pricing often requires domain expertise beyond statistical modeling
