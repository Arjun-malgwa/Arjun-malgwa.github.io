Flood Risk Analytics for Montpelier, VT

Sep 2024 - Dec 2024 | Capstone Project

Challenge & Objective

Insurance companies struggle to accurately identify and zone flood-risk areas, leading to under-priced policies and coverage gaps. This capstone project aimed to develop a data-driven approach to map high-risk zones near the Winooski River in Montpelier, VT, and optimize insurance premiums based on geographic risk factors.

Approach & Methodology

1. Risk Zone Identification

Built a K-means clustering model using geospatial data (elevation, proximity to water bodies) to classify high-risk flood zones. Integrated FEMA flood data and Precisely APIs to identify critical areas near the Winooski River and low-elevation regions with heightened vulnerability.

2. Underinsurance Detection

Designed an Insurance-to-Value (ITV) model to flag underinsured properties in flood-prone zones. This analysis uncovered significant coverage gaps in high-value properties, enabling targeted upselling opportunities and risk mitigation strategies.

3. Premium Optimization

Developed a Random Forest Regressor to predict localized premium ranges, balancing affordability for policyholders with profitability for the insurer. The model incorporated risk zone classification, property features, and historical claims data.

Results & Impact

  • Coverage Gap Identification: Uncovered 25%+ coverage gaps in high-value properties within flood-prone zones
  • Customer Satisfaction: 15% improvement in policyholder satisfaction through more accurate and fair premium pricing
  • Risk Mitigation: Successfully classified critical flood-risk areas near Winooski River and low-elevation regions
  • Business Value: Targeted upselling opportunities for underinsured properties and optimized premium ranges

Tech Stack

Python K-means Clustering Random Forest Geospatial Analysis FEMA Data Precisely APIs Pandas Scikit-learn

Key Learnings

  • Geospatial data integration requires careful handling of coordinate systems and projections
  • K-means is effective for geographic zoning but requires domain expertise to validate cluster boundaries
  • Ensemble methods like Random Forest outperform linear models for complex, multi-feature insurance pricing
  • Cross-validation with time-based splits is critical for predictive models in finance
