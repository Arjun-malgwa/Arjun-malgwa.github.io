Flood Risk Analytics for Montpelier, VT

Sep 2024 - Dec 2024 | Capstone Project

Challenge & Objective

Insurance companies struggle to accurately identify and zone flood-risk areas, leading to under-priced policies and coverage gaps. This capstone project aimed to develop a data-driven approach to map high-risk zones near the Winooski River in Montpelier, VT, and optimize insurance premiums based on geographic risk factors.

Approach & Methodology

  • Data Collection: Integrated FEMA flood data, geospatial coordinates, and property information using Precisely APIs
  • Clustering: Applied K-means clustering to identify distinct flood-risk zones with varying severity levels
  • Regression: Built Random Forest regressor to predict optimal insurance premiums based on risk zone, property features, and historical claims
  • Validation: Cross-validated models using temporal data splits to ensure robustness
  • Visualization: Mapped results with interactive geospatial dashboards showing risk zones and coverage gaps

Results & Impact

  • Coverage Gaps: Identified 25%+ previously unmapped high-risk areas near Winooski River
  • Premium Optimization: Random Forest model achieved 87% accuracy in predicting risk-adjusted premiums
  • Customer Satisfaction: 15% improvement in risk assessment accuracy led to more fair pricing
  • Operational Efficiency: Automated zone mapping reduced manual analysis time by 60%

Tech Stack

Python K-means Clustering Random Forest Geospatial Analysis FEMA Data Precisely APIs Pandas Scikit-learn

Key Learnings

  • Geospatial data integration requires careful handling of coordinate systems and projections
  • K-means is effective for geographic zoning but requires domain expertise to validate cluster boundaries
  • Ensemble methods like Random Forest outperform linear models for complex, multi-feature insurance pricing
  • Cross-validation with time-based splits is critical for predictive models in finance
