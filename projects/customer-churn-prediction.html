Customer Churn Prediction for Online Store

Mar 2024 | Kaggle Competition - #1 Ranking

Challenge & Objective

An e-commerce platform faced a 90% customer churn rate, resulting in massive revenue loss. The challenge was to build a predictive model to identify at-risk customers before they leave, enabling proactive retention campaigns. This project competed against 24 other teams in a Kaggle competition.

Approach & Methodology

1. Predictive Model Development

Developed a comprehensive predictive model to identify customers at risk of not returning, addressing a 90% churn rate. The model was designed to enable proactive customer retention campaigns and improve CLV (Customer Lifetime Value).

2. Feature Analysis & Importance

Analyzed key variables affecting churn through correlation analysis and XGBoost importance plots. Emphasized the critical importance of active customer engagement and identified behavioral patterns distinguishing at-risk from loyal customers.

3. Model Robustness & Validation

Achieved model robustness and reproducibility using stratified cross-validation and comprehensive hyperparameter tuning. Prioritized accuracy and precision in churn prediction to minimize false positives in retention campaigns.

Results & Impact

  • Kaggle Competition: Secured #1 Rank in STAT 642 Final Project Kaggle Competition, outperforming 24 other groups
  • Model Performance: Achieved exceptional accuracy and precision in churn prediction
  • Business Impact: Identified at-risk customers enabling proactive retention strategies
  • Innovation: Innovative approach to customer churn prediction and retention strategies

Tech Stack

R XGBoost Feature Engineering Hyperparameter Tuning Cross-Validation RStudio Tidyverse Caret

Key Learnings

  • Feature engineering is often more impactful than algorithm selection
  • RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis provides powerful predictors for churn
  • Cross-validation prevents overfitting and ensures model generalization
  • Ensemble methods like XGBoost excel at capturing non-linear patterns in customer behavior
  • Class imbalance handling is critical when predicting rare events
