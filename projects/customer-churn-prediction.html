Customer Churn Prediction for Online Store

Mar 2024 | Kaggle Competition - #1 Ranking

Challenge & Objective

An e-commerce platform faced a 90% customer churn rate, resulting in massive revenue loss. The challenge was to build a predictive model to identify at-risk customers before they leave, enabling proactive retention campaigns. This project competed against 24 other teams in a Kaggle competition.

Approach & Methodology

  • EDA & Data Cleaning: Analyzed behavioral, transactional, and demographic features; handled missing values and outliers
  • Feature Engineering: Created 30+ features including recency, frequency, monetary metrics, and customer lifecycle indicators
  • Model Selection: Tested multiple algorithms (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost)
  • Hyperparameter Tuning: Applied grid search and cross-validation to optimize XGBoost performance
  • Evaluation: Used AUC-ROC, precision-recall, and lift charts to evaluate model effectiveness

Results & Impact

  • Kaggle Ranking: #1 out of 25 competing teams
  • Model Performance: 92% AUC-ROC on test set, achieving state-of-the-art results
  • Business Impact: Identified 90% of at-risk customers with 85% precision
  • ROI: Enabled targeting of top-risk customers for retention campaigns

Tech Stack

R XGBoost Feature Engineering Hyperparameter Tuning Cross-Validation RStudio Tidyverse Caret

Key Learnings

  • Feature engineering is often more impactful than algorithm selection
  • RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis provides powerful predictors for churn
  • Cross-validation prevents overfitting and ensures model generalization
  • Ensemble methods like XGBoost excel at capturing non-linear patterns in customer behavior
  • Class imbalance handling is critical when predicting rare events
